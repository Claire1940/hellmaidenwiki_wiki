<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Deck-Building & Card Synergy">

The defining feature of combat is your deck. Your deck determines your offensive output, defensive capabilities, and utility actions. Cards are divided into three primary categories: Weapon Cards, Mod Cards, and Spirit Cards. Understanding how these categories interact is the key to surviving high-density enemy waves.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Weapon Cards">
    - **Core Offense**: Defines your primary attack style and base damage output.
    - **Signature Weapons**: Choose from 4 distinct starting weapons in Early Access.
    - **Upgradable**: Can be enhanced directly during runs.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Mod Cards">
    - **Behavior Modification**: Alters how your Weapon Cards behave.
    - **Stat Boosts**: Adds elemental damage, extra projectiles, or pierce effects.
    - **Adaptability**: Swap mods on the fly to counter specific enemy types.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Spirit Cards">
    - **Passive Perks**: Provides passive bonuses and defensive utility.
    - **Status Effects**: Inflicts burn, freeze, or vulnerability on targets.
    - **Synergy Enablers**: Boosts the effectiveness of matching weapon attributes.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

To build an effective deck, you must avoid simply picking the rarest cards. Instead, focus on creating cohesive synergies. For instance, pairing a fast-attacking Weapon Card with Mod Cards that apply on-hit status effects, such as freeze or burn, can freeze entire screenfuls of demons in seconds.

| Card Type | Primary Function | Best Combined With | Synergy Rating |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Weapon Cards | Direct damage delivery | High-damage Mod Cards | ★★★★★ |
| Mod Cards | Attack pattern alteration | High-rate-of-fire weapons | ★★★★☆ |
| Spirit Cards | Passive stat enhancement | Defensive utility perks | ★★★★☆ |

<Callout type="tip" title="Deck Optimization">
Do not over-inflate your deck with too many different cards. Keeping your deck lean ensures that your most powerful weapon and mod combinations are drawn more frequently during intense combat phases.
</Callout>

</Section>
