<Section icon={Keyboard} title="Keyboard and Mouse Input Layout">

Understanding the **Hell Maiden controls** is the first step toward surviving the depths of Limbo. Unlike many traditional horde-survival games where attacks are purely automated based on proximity, this title requires players to actively point their weapons toward the threat. This "twin-stick" approach on a keyboard and mouse setup offers the highest precision for focused damage builds like the Blazing Quill or Winged Lyric.

| Key | Action | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **W, A, S, D** | Movement | Move Dante in eight directions across the Circle. |
| **Mouse Cursor** | Aiming | Directs all automatic attacks toward the pointer. |
| **Spacebar** | Dash | A quick burst of speed with temporary invincibility. |
| **E / Right Click** | Use Spell | Activates the signature Poet Spell (e.g., Dante's Inferno). |
| **Tab / I** | Open Deck | View and rearrange your current Spirit and Mod Cards. |
| **Esc** | Pause Menu | Access settings, check achievements, or quit the run. |

<Callout type="tip" title="Aiming Strategy">
Because Dante attacks automatically, your primary focus should be "kiting." Keep your mouse cursor pointed at the densest part of the enemy wave while moving Dante in the opposite direction to maintain a safe distance.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Gamepad2} title="Controller and Gamepad Configuration">

For players who prefer a more relaxed or "bullet-hell" style experience, the **Hell Maiden controls** for gamepads are highly intuitive. The game uses a standard twin-stick shooter layout. This is particularly effective for "Projectile Pressure" builds where you need to constantly rotate your fire to clear surrounding mobs.

| Button (Xbox/PS) | Action | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Left Stick** | Character Movement | High |
| **Right Stick** | Aiming Direction | High |
| **RB / R1 / B** | Dash / Evade | Critical |
| **LB / L1 / A** | Activate Spell | Moderate |
| **View / Select** | Deck Management | Moderate |
| **Menu / Start** | Pause Game | Low |

<Callout type="warning" title="Controller Aiming">
Ensure your Right Stick is always tilted in a direction. If the stick returns to the neutral position, Dante may stop firing accurately at specific targets, which can be fatal during the 12-minute wave peaks.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering Combat and Movement Mechanics">

Survival in Hell Maiden depends on how well you can balance movement and offensive aiming. The 12-minute timer in Circles like Limbo and Lust introduces increasingly dense waves that require more than just walking in circles. Mastering the **Hell Maiden controls** means learning the rhythm of the dash and the timing of your Poet's Spell.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Defensive Dash">
    The dash is your most important survival tool. It allows Dante to pass through enemies and projectiles without taking damage. Never use your last dash charge just to move faster; always keep one available for escaping a "surrounded" state or dodging Queen Minos's area-of-effect (AoE) attacks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Manual Aiming Priority">
    Priority targets like Elite Vipers or Succubi should be focused down first. Use your mouse or Right Stick to track these specific enemies while your movement (WASD/Left Stick) keeps you clear of the general horde.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Activating Temples">
    While moving, you will encounter Temples. Activating these requires you to stay within their vicinity for a short duration. Use your movement controls to "orbit" the Temple, keeping enemies on the outside of your circle while staying close enough to trigger the progress bar.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Advanced Tactic">
You can dash *through* a boss. If Queen Minos traps you against the edge of the arena, dashing directly through her model is often safer than trying to run around the projectile wall.
</Callout>

| **Auto-Fire** | Passive | Constant DPS as long as a target is in range/aim. |
| **Spell Burst** | Active | Massive screen clear; use when movement is restricted. |

<Section icon={Settings} title="Interface and Deck Management Controls">

A unique aspect of the **Hell Maiden controls** is the ability to rearrange your "hand" of Spirit Cards during a run. This isn't just for organization; many Mod Cards provide bonuses based on their position (e.g., "increases damage of the weapon to the right").

**Rearranging Cards:**
- **PC**: Click and drag a Spirit Card or Mod to a different slot.
- **Controller**: Use the d-pad or stick to highlight a card, press the confirm button to "pick up," and move it to the desired slot.

<Callout type="info" title="Merging Mods">
When you collect duplicate Mod Cards, the game will prompt you to merge them. This is usually done through the level-up screen, but keeping your deck organized via the Tab/Deck menu ensures you are always getting the maximum Level 3 bonuses on your primary weapon.
</Callout>

| Slot Type | Max Capacity | Control Interaction |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Weapon Cards** | 4 Slots | Primary damage sources; can be swapped/reordered. |
| **Mod Cards** | 3 per Weapon | Attached to weapons; merging improves rarity/stats. |
| **Spell Slot** | 1 Slot | Tied to the specific Poet (Dante, Virgil, etc.). |

</Section>
