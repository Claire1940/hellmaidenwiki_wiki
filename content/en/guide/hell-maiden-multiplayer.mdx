<Section icon={Swords} title="How Deck-Building Mimics Multiplayer Synergies">

Even without a dedicated co-op partner, the deck-building mechanics in Hell Maiden allow you to create complex synergies that feel like coordinating with a teammate. By combining different card types, you can build a versatile loadout capable of handling both massive single-target bosses and overwhelming waves of minor demons. Understanding the layout of your hand is crucial, as certain cards apply bonuses to adjacent weapons.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Weapon Cards">
    The core of your offensive arsenal. You can hold up to four unique weapon cards per run, ranging from close-range slashes to piercing fireballs.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Mod Cards">
    These cards modify and enhance your weapon cards. They are categorized into five types: boost, effect, spread, miracle, and curse.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Spirit Cards">
    Passive bonuses that grant overall stat increases, such as critical rate boosts, damage reduction, and increased movement speed.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Beatrice's Charms">
    Temporary run-specific power-ups that come in bronze, silver, and gold rarities, offering utility like increased magnetic pull or attack size.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

To maximize your efficiency, you must master the art of card positioning. For example, placing a Spread Mod Card in a central slot will distribute its bonuses to the weapons on both its left and right. This spatial puzzle adds a layer of tactical planning that keeps solo runs incredibly dynamic.

| Mod Type | Core Mechanic | Synergy Level | Optimal Weapon Positioning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Boost | Upgrades single stat | Moderate | Any weapon slot |
| Effect | Adds status (burn/poison) | High | Rapid-fire weapons |
| Spread | Buffs adjacent weapons | Very High | Central weapon slots |
| Miracle | Multiple powerful boosts | Excellent | Signature weapons |
| Curse | High risk, high reward | Situational | Specialized builds |

<Callout type="tip" title="Card Merging">
You can merge two identical mod cards of the same level to upgrade them up to level three. Watch for the orange glow in your hand to identify mergeable cards.
</Callout>

</Section>
