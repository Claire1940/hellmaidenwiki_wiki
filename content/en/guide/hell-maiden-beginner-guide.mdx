<Section icon={Zap} title="Mastering Card Merging and Modifiers">

The most unique aspect of the hell maiden experience is the card system. Unlike traditional rogue-likes where you simply pick a perk, this game allows you to slot modification cards into specific weapons and merge duplicate cards to increase their level. Each weapon has a limited number of slots, so you must be strategic about which effects you apply to your primary damage dealer versus your secondary support weapons.

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Management">
Merging cards consumes the base cards to create a higher-level version. Ensure you have a secondary source of damage before merging your primary weapon's only speed modifier.
</Callout>

| Card Name | Primary Effect | Best Synergy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sataris Path** | +100% Damage / -Attack Speed | Swift Strikes |
| **Swift Strikes** | Increases Attack Speed | Sataris Path |
| **Kindled Embers** | Increases Projectile Count | Venus's Favor |
| **Flare Up** | 30% Chance for Explosions | High Damage Builds |
| **Eternal Prestige** | 5% Chance to Absorb HP | Fast Attacking Weapons |

When building your loadout, look for **Modification Cards** (often blue or gold). These can be placed between weapon cards to apply their effects to multiple adjacent items. For example, **Venus's Favor** increases the projectile count of adjacent weapons, making it a "must-have" for any build relying on the Blazing Quill or Kindled Embers.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Collect Duplicates">
    Pick up the same card multiple times from level-ups or chests to prepare for a merge.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Open Inventory">
    Access the card management screen to view your current weapon slots and modifiers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Drag and Drop">
    Drag one card onto another of the same type and level to create a Level 2 or Level 3 version.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Optimize Slots">
    Place your highest-level modifiers next to your strongest weapons to maximize the stat bonuses.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Survival Tactics and Enemy Behavior">

In hell maiden, survival is just as important as damage. The game will frequently throw "Suicide Bombers" and "Elite Slayers" at you that can end a run in seconds if you get cornered. Understanding enemy indicators and maintaining a safe distance is the hallmark of a pro player. Projectile enemies are particularly dangerous because their shots can be hard to see amidst the visual chaos of your own fire effects.

<Callout type="info" title="Defensive Stats">
Prioritize **General Defense** and **Melee Resistance** if you find yourself getting hit by mobs. A defense stat of 13% or higher significantly increases your room for error during boss fights.
</Callout>

**Key Defensive Strategies:**
- **Kiting**: Always move in a wide circle around the center of the map to group enemies together for AOE attacks.
- **Dash Management**: Save your dash for escaping "Body Blocks" where enemies surround you completely.
- **Ultimate Timing**: Use Dante's **Inferno Ultimate** not just for damage, but for the brief period of invulnerability and screen clearing it provides.
- **XP Gathering**: Don't leave experience gems on the ground for too long. Use a **Magnet** power-up or the **Fatal Yield** card to stay ahead of the level curve.

| Enemy Type | Danger Level | Best Counter |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Caterpies** | Low | Any AOE attack |
| **Suicide Bombers** | High | Dash away, ranged fire |
| **Ranged Imps** | Medium | Projectile Defense, constant movement |
| **Elite Slayers** | Very High | Ultimate ability, kiting |

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Boss Strategy: Defeating Minos">

The culmination of the Limbo circle is the battle against **Minos**, the Queen of the Underworld. This fight is a test of your positioning and build quality. Minos uses a variety of attacks, including shotgun-style projectile blasts and floor-based lasers. Because the Blazing Quill requires you to be relatively close to the target, you must master the "hit and run" tactic to avoid her devastating close-range bursts.

<Callout type="tip" title="Minos Strategy">
Stay slightly to the side of Minos rather than directly in front of her. Most of her projectile attacks fire in a cone directly toward your current position.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Phase One: Projectiles">
    Dodge the green shotgun blasts by moving laterally. Do not dash toward her during this phase.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Phase Two: Lasers">
    Watch for the floor indicators. Move to the gaps between the beams immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Phase Three: Last Rights">
    Minos will fire a series of high-damage projectiles in a circular pattern. Stay at mid-range to easily find the gaps.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Finish">
    Once her health is low, trigger your **Inferno Ultimate** to burn through her remaining HP while ignoring minor chip damage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Minos Attack | Damage Type | How to Avoid |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shotgun Blast** | Projectile | Lateral movement |
| **Hellfire Lasers** | AOE / Floor | Stand in safe zones |
| **Summon Mobs** | Melee | Use AOE/Explosions |
| **Last Rights** | Heavy Projectile | Precise kiting at distance |
