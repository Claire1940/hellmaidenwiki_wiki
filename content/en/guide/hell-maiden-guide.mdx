The primary loop involves collecting experience orbs to draw cards. These cards are categorized into weapons and modifiers. You can hold up to four unique weapons at a time, and each weapon can be augmented with several mod cards. Positioning matters, as some "Spread" cards affect adjacent weapons, requiring careful planning of your hand layout.

| Card Category | Primary Function | Strategic Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Weapon** | Core attack method | Determines your primary damage type and range |
| **Boost** | Stat enhancement | Increases flat damage, size, or projectile speed |
| **Effect** | Status application | Adds Burn, Slow, or Poison to weapon hits |
| **Spread** | Multi-target buff | Buffs multiple weapons based on hand position |
| **Miracle** | Rare power-up | Provides multiple high-tier stat increases |
| **Curse** | High risk/reward | Massive buffs at the cost of speed or health |

<Callout type="tip" title="Early Game Priority">
Focus on increasing your **Attack Size** and **Projectile Count** in the first five minutes. This allows you to clear the initial "snake" swarms efficiently and gather experience orbs without getting boxed in.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Card Modification and Merging System">

The depth of any **hell maiden guide** lies in mastering the merging mechanic. When you possess two identical cards of the same level (indicated by the color of the card border), you can drag one onto the other to evolve it. This process not only increases the base stats of the mod but also changes its appearance and effectiveness significantly.

| Mod Level | Border Color | Stat Multiplier | Merge Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1** | Bronze | Baseline | Found in standard level-up draws |
| **Level 2** | Silver | 1.5x - 2.0x | Two Level 1 cards or rare drops |
| **Level 3** | Gold | 3.0x+ | Two Level 2 cards |

Modifiers like "Flare Up" provide a percentage chance to cause explosions on kill. At Level 1, this might be a 30% chance for 30% damage. By the time you reach Level 3, the proc rate jumps to 80%, turning every enemy death into a chain reaction of area-of-effect damage.

  </Card>
  <Card title="Effect Cards">
    - **Burn**: Deals damage over time to tanky elites.
    - **Slow**: Essential for kitting fast-moving bosses.
    - **Life Steal**: Rare chance to recover HP on hit.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Spread Cards">
    - **Adjacent Buff**: Increases damage of cards to the left/right.
    - **Global Boost**: Provides smaller buffs to all weapons.

<Callout type="warning" title="Merger Limits">
You can only merge two cards of the same level. If you have a Level 2 card and draw a Level 1 version, they cannot be combined. Always keep an eye on your inventory space to avoid cluttering your hand with un-mergeable duplicates.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Character Unlocks and Signature Weapons">

Progression in Limbo is marked by rescuing other "Poets of Limbo." Each character brings a unique signature weapon and a set of blessings that alter the pool of available cards for future runs. Dante is the starting character, but Virgil and Horus offer vastly different playstyles that are essential for tackling higher difficulty levels.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Master Dante">
    Utilize the **Blazing Quill** to clear the first few levels. Focus on fire-based modifications and the "Flare Up" explosion mod to handle large crowds.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Unlock Virgil">
    After surviving the initial boss encounter, Virgil's blessing becomes available. Virgil specializes in projectile-based combat with the **Wayfinder** weapon, which shoots swerving projectiles.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Rescue Horus">
    Locate Horus on the map during a run. You must protect her from waves of enemies until the timer expires. Once rescued, she provides high-damage melee blessings and the **Eternal Prestige** mod.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Character | Signature Weapon | Playstyle | Key Blessing |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dante** | Blazing Quill | Fire / AoE | Increased Burn damage and duration |
| **Virgil** | Wayfinder | Projectile / Kiting | Extra projectile count for all weapons |
| **Horus** | Spear / Melee | High Burst / Life Steal | HP absorption and Elite damage bonus |

<Callout type="success" title="Horus Synergy">
The **Eternal Prestige** mod from Horus is a game-changer. It grants a 5% to 15% chance to absorb HP from enemies. Pairing this with a high-attack-speed weapon like the lightning strike makes you nearly invincible in high-density waves.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Survival Tactics and Boss Strategies">

Bosses in this magical girl survival game act as massive DPS checks. They often feature bullet-hell patterns and high movement speed. Survival depends on your ability to maintain distance while maximizing your "spell" uptime. Spells are powerful abilities triggered by the Q key that often clear the screen or provide temporary invulnerability.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Advanced Build: The Moon and Lightning Combo">

For players looking to break the meta, the "Selenic Bloom" (Moon) and Lightning strike combination offers unparalleled screen clearance. The Moon weapon creates a lingering AoE ring that heals the player if combined with Horus's blessings. Meanwhile, the Lightning strike can be modified with "Invigorate" to deal over 300% damage.

| Weapon Combo | Mod Requirements | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Selenic Bloom** | Projectile Count + Life Steal | Multiple healing zones on screen |
| **Lightning Bolt** | Invigorate + Swift Strikes | High-speed, massive damage strikes |
| **Wayfinder** | Size + Spread | Massive screen coverage for clearing mobs |

The key to this build is the **Swift Strikes** mod, which negates the attack speed penalty of heavy damage modifiers like "Invigorate." This allows your lightning bolts to strike at standard speeds while maintaining a 300% damage multiplier, effectively melting elite enemies in seconds.

As of the 2026 meta, stacking **Experience Gain** charms early in the run (first 10 levels) is more effective than taking immediate damage buffs. Reaching Level 50 before the final boss ensures you have enough card slots to maximize your build potential.
</Callout>
