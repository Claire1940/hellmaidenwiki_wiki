When building your hand, remember that positional effects are vital. Some Mod cards provide bonuses to adjacent weapons or weapons to their right. Always rearrange your hand to ensure your primary boss-killing weapon receives the maximum possible buffs from your Mod cards.

<Callout type="tip" title="Mod Merging Strategy">
Always aim to merge duplicate Mod cards into a **Level 3** version. A single Level 3 Mod is significantly more powerful than three Level 1 copies spread across different weapons. For the Minos fight, prioritize Level 3 Damage and Projectile Count mods.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Map} title="Step-by-Step Survival and Boss Route">

Reaching **hell maiden minos** is a journey that starts the moment you spawn in Limbo. You must utilize the 12-minute survival window to maximize your power.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Early Resource Gathering">
    Focus on collecting experience orbs from defeated Imps and Skeletons. Your priority in the first 3 minutes is to fill all four Weapon slots to ensure you have a diverse range of attacks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Poet Rescue and Unlocks">
    Locate and rescue Horace and Homer within Limbo. Saving these Poets expands your card pool, adding the Carpe Diem and Winged Lyric weapons to your available level-up choices.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Temple Activation">
    Explore the map to find and activate Temples. Activating these provides run-based benefits and is required for the Pious Pilgrim achievement (12 Temples in one run). Do this before the 10-minute mark when enemy density peaks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Mod Optimization">
    By the 10-minute mark, you should have at least one Weapon card equipped with three Level 3 Mods. This "Perfect Hand" setup is essential for melting Minos's high health bar.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="The Final Confrontation">
    Once the timer hits 12 minutes, Queen Minos will spawn. Keep your distance, watch for the red floor telegraphs, and only use your dash when a projectile wall leaves no other gap.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Health Preservation">
Avoid unnecessary contact damage during the 12-minute survival phase. Entering the Minos fight with low health is extremely dangerous, as her projectile patterns can be difficult to navigate without a safety margin.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Queen Minos Attack Patterns and Counters">

Understanding the telegraphed behavior of **hell maiden minos** is the difference between victory and a failed run. She cycles through several distinct phases that test your reflexes and positioning.

| Attack Pattern | Description | Recommended Counter |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Infernal Spiral** | Circular waves of projectiles | Move laterally/circle the boss |
| **Limbo Strike** | Large AoE floor telegraphs | Dash out of the red zone immediately |
| **Soul Summon** | Spawns minor enemies to distract | Use area-clearing secondary weapons |
| **Abyssal Gate** | Restricts arena movement | Save Poet Spell for this phase |

The most dangerous part of the fight occurs when Minos combines her projectile spirals with floor strikes. During these moments, do not panic-dash. Instead, look for the "safe lanes" between the projectiles and only use your dash to cross over a telegraphed strike zone.

<Callout type="info" title="Poet Spells">
Dante's Inferno or Virgil's Star Sign ☆ Pleiades can clear the screen of projectiles. Use these defensively when you feel overwhelmed by the bullet-hell patterns.
</Callout>

</Section>
