<Section icon={Sparkles} title="A and B Tier: Reliable Alternatives">

Not every run provides S-tier cards immediately. Mastering the **hell maiden tier list** involves knowing how to utilize A and B tier weapons to bridge the gap until your build is complete. A-tier weapons are generally powerful but lack the "set and forget" ease of S-tier picks.

**Wrath of Astrap** is a prime example of an A-tier weapon. It provides powerful lightning strikes that automatically target enemies. While it is incredibly effective at clearing small clusters and focusing on bosses, it doesn't provide the same defensive utility as Pirate Dance. Similarly, **Blistering Wake** turns your dash into a weapon by leaving a trail of fire. It is highly effective for crowd control, but it requires the player to constantly "run laps" around the arena, which can be tedious in longer sessions.

<Callout type="warning" title="Aiming Difficulty">
**Blade of Augustus** (B-Tier) has a unique hitbox issue. Increasing the size of the sword can actually make it harder to hit enemies that are directly on top of you, as the damage is focused on the blade's edge and tip.
</Callout>

Even in a well-balanced game like hell maiden, some weapons fall short of the mark. These are typically weapons that are either too difficult to aim or provide negligible damage compared to the effort required to use them.

**Wayfinder** is widely considered the worst weapon in the current demo. It fires a curved projectile that is notoriously difficult to aim accurately. Similarly, **Kindled Embers** creates a magical fire circle around the player, but the damage output is often too low to prevent enemies from walking right through it.
