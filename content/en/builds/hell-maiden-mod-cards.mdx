<Section icon={Map} title="Understanding Hell Maiden Mod Cards Mechanics">

In the gothic world of Hell Maiden, your survival depends entirely on how you construct your deck of Spirit Cards during a run. While Weapon Cards define your active attacks, **hell maiden mod cards** serve as the engine that drives your DPS and utility. Every time you level up by collecting experience orbs in the Circles of Hell, you are presented with choices that can either add a new weapon to your hand or attach a modifier to an existing one.

Your hand has a strict limit: four Weapon Card slots. Each of these weapons can support up to three Mod Cards. This creates a total of 12 modifier slots that must be managed carefully. Unlike other horde survival games where upgrades are generic, Hell Maiden requires you to think about which specific weapon receives which buff.

| Mod Category | Primary Function | Scaling Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Offensive** | Increases raw damage and critical hit chance | High for Bosses |
| **Utility** | Boosts attack speed and projectile count | High for Hordes |
| **Positional** | Grants bonuses based on card placement | Medium |
| **Status** | Applies burn, slow, or other debuffs | Contextual |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
Always fill your four Weapon Card slots before focusing heavily on Mod Cards. Having a variety of coverage (Area, Single Target, Projectile) is safer than having one highly modded weapon and empty slots.
</Callout>

### The Synergy Between Weapons and Mods
Each Poet you rescue, such as Horace or Ovid, introduces new **hell maiden mod cards** into the loot pool. For example, Virgil’s "Wayfinder" weapon pairs exceptionally well with mods that increase projectile duration, while Dante’s "Blazing Quill" benefits most from raw damage and attack speed. Understanding these natural synergies is the first step toward clearing the Lust circle.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Merging System: Path to Level 3">

One of the most unique aspects of **hell maiden mod cards** is the merging system. Instead of simply collecting dozens of weak buffs, you are encouraged to find duplicates. Merging is the only way to reach the "Perfect Hand" achievement and maximize your damage output for the final encounter with Queen Minos or the Malebranche bosses.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify Duplicates">
    When you level up, the game will highlight cards you already possess. If you select an identical Mod Card that is already attached to a weapon, it will automatically merge.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Track Level Milestones">
    Each mod starts at Level 1. You need two Level 1s to make a Level 2, and two Level 2s (or equivalent duplicates) to reach the coveted Level 3 status.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Prioritize Core Weapons">
    Don't spread your merges across all four weapons. Focus on getting your primary damage dealer (like Winged Lyric or Codex Amoris) to three Level 3 mods first.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Finalize the Hand">
    Once a mod reaches Level 3, its icon will glow. A full hand of 12 Level 3 mods makes Dante virtually unstoppable, even in the deepest parts of Lust.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Management">
Merging consumes a level-up choice. Sometimes it is better to take a new Level 1 mod that provides a missing utility (like health regen or area size) than to push a damage mod from Level 2 to Level 3.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Optimal Card Positioning and Synergies">

The horizontal layout of your hand isn't just for show. Several **hell maiden mod cards** feature "Positional" mechanics. These mods check the cards to their left or right and apply bonuses accordingly. If you arrange your hand randomly, you are leaving massive amounts of free DPS on the table.

| Position | Role | Recommended Mod Type |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Slot 1 (Far Left)** | Buffer | Mods that boost "All cards to the right" |
| **Slot 2 & 3** | Main DPS | Pure damage, Speed, and Projectiles |
| **Slot 4 (Far Right)** | Utility/Finisher | Status effects or "Adjacent left" buffs |

<Callout type="success" title="The Right-Side Meta">
The "Right-Hand Lead" mod is currently one of the strongest in Early Access. By placing it on a weapon in Slot 1, you can significantly boost your heaviest hitter in Slot 2.
</Callout>

### Strategic Arrangements
1. **The Chain Build**: Use mods that provide bonuses to the "Next Card." Arrange your weapons from fastest to slowest to ensure the slower, harder-hitting weapons benefit from the speed buffs of the earlier slots.
2. **The Sandwich Build**: Place a weapon with "Adjacent Bonus" mods in Slot 2 or 3. This allows a single mod to buff two different weapons simultaneously, effectively doubling the value of that card slot.

</Section>
