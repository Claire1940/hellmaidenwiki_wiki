Spirit cards are currently categorized into two distinct groups. Understanding the relationship between these two is the first step to mastering the game. Weapon cards occupy the four main slots in your hand, while mod cards are attached to these weapons to enhance their performance.

| Card Category | Primary Function | Maximum Slots | Upgrade Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Weapon Cards** | Primary source of damage and attack patterns | 4 Slots | Leveling up and selecting duplicates |
| **Mod Cards** | Stat boosts, projectiles, and utility effects | Varies | Merging identical cards to Level 3 |
| **Spell Cards** | Screen-clearing ultimate abilities | 1 Slot | Character-specific or Poet rewards |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">

<Section icon={Shield} title="Mod Cards and Merging Mechanics">

Mod cards are the "engine" of your build. While weapons provide the delivery system, mods determine the damage, speed, and behavior of those attacks. One of the most important mechanics involving **hell maiden spirit cards** is the merging system. You cannot simply find a Level 3 card; you must build it.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Collect Duplicates">
    When you level up, you will often be presented with mod cards you already own. Selecting a duplicate will add it to your inventory for that specific weapon.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Check Level Requirements">
    It takes two Level 1 cards to create a Level 2 card, and two Level 2 cards (or a total of four Level 1s) to reach the coveted Level 3.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Merge for Power Spikes">
    Once you have enough duplicates, the game allows you to merge them. Level 3 mods often gain secondary effects or massive stat multipliers that are essential for the 12-minute survival mark.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Mod Type | Common Effects | Priority Level | Merging Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Offensive** | Damage, Crit Chance, Attack Speed | **Highest** | Massive damage scaling |
| **Utility** | Projectile Count, Area of Effect | **High** | Improved screen clearing |
| **Defensive** | Healing Support, Damage Reduction | **Medium** | Essential for "Vital Endurance" runs |
| **Positional** | Buffs to adjacent or specific slots | **Strategic** | Enables complex synergies |

<Callout type="warning" title="Important">
Do not spread your mod cards across all four weapons too thinly. It is much more effective to have one weapon with three Level 3 mods than four weapons with Level 1 mods.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Map} title="Optimal Card Positioning and Synergies">

The layout of your hand is just as important as the cards themselves. Some of the most powerful **hell maiden spirit cards** have "Positional Effects." These cards provide bonuses based on where they are placed relative to other cards in your four-slot hand.

For example, a mod might say "Increases damage of the weapon to the right by 20%." If you place your strongest weapon in the leftmost slot, that mod is effectively wasted. You must constantly rearrange your hand as you acquire new positional mods to ensure you are maximizing your output.

| Position Strategy | How It Works | Recommended Weapons |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Core Lead** | Place your primary damage dealer in slot 2 or 3 to receive adjacent buffs. | Carpe Diem, Winged Lyric |
| **The Buffer End** | Use slot 1 or 4 for weapons that primarily exist to apply status or positional buffs. | Codex Amoris, Wayfinder |
| **The Symmetrical Build** | Use mods that buff both adjacent slots, placing your best weapons in the middle. | Blazing Quill, Wayfinder |

<Callout type="info" title="Positional Tip">
Always read the fine print on Mod cards. Some bonuses only apply if the weapon is "to the right" or "adjacent," while others might buff the "entire hand" but at a lower percentage.
</Callout>

</Section>
