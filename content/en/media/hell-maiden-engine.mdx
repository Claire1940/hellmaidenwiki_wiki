<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Mastering the Hell Maiden Engine: Deck Synergies">

At the core of the **hell maiden engine** is the deck-building interface. Players often make the mistake of filling every slot with high-damage weapons, leading to builds that collapse under pressure. The engine rewards a balanced approach that considers weapon speed, projectile count, and support mods.

| Card Type | Primary Function | Adjacency Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Weapon Cards** | Direct damage output | Receives buffs from Mod Cards |
| **Spirit Cards** | Passive stat increases | Can trigger secondary effects |
| **Mod Cards** | Enhances Weapon Cards | Must be adjacent to a weapon |
| **Ultimate Cards** | High-impact screen clears | Requires Poet rescue to unlock |
| **Signature Weapons** | Unique playstyle anchors | Scaled by specific Poet perks |

The placement of these cards is critical. Some Mod Cards only affect the weapon directly to their left or right. If you place a powerful "Multi-Shot" mod in a slot that isn't touching a compatible weapon, you are effectively wasting a slot. The engine is designed to make you constantly re-evaluate your hand as you progress through the circles of hell.

  <Card title="The Sustain Build">
    Focuses on high-frequency, low-damage attacks like **Orb** or **Moon Spheres**. When paired with healing-per-hit mods, this creates a "healing engine" that keeps Dante alive during crowded encounters.
  </Card>
  <Card title="The Control Build">
    Utilizes **Earth Spikes** and **Lightning** to stun or slow enemies. This build prioritizes battlefield management, ensuring that Dante is never cornered by the encroaching hordes.

</CardGrid>

<Callout type="warning" title="Deck Organization">

<Section icon={Target} title="The Poet Rescue System">

Progression in Hell Maiden is inextricably linked to the Poets of Limbo. You are not just fighting to survive; you are fighting to liberate the great thinkers of antiquity. Rescuing these characters is the only way to expand your tactical options in the **hell maiden engine**.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate the Poet">
    Each level contains a specific quest or hidden area where a poet is held. Keep an eye on the mini-map for unique icons indicating their location.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Clear the Challenge">
    Poets are usually guarded by elite "Fiends" or require a specific survival time to unlock. You must defeat these guardians to initiate the rescue.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Befriend and Unlock">
    Once rescued, poets return to your hub. Talking to them unlocks 5 new abilities per poet, as well as unique perks that modify your base stats.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Integrate Signature Gear">
    After reaching certain friendship milestones, poets grant access to their signature weapons, which often define high-level meta builds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Poet | Historical Role | Key Unlock | Playstyle Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Homer** | Epic Poet | Legendary Bow | Long-range precision |
| **Ovid** | Metamorphoses | Transformation Mod | Adaptive ability shifting |
| **Horace** | Satirist | Critical Strike Perk | High-risk, high-reward |
| **Lucan** | Pharsalia | Bleed/DoT Effects | Sustained pressure |

<Section icon={Shield} title="Survival Tactics and Boss Mechanics">

The **hell maiden engine** features two primary circles of hell in its current Early Access state, each culminating in a brutal boss fight. These bosses are not just obstacles; they are the sources of your most powerful abilities. When you defeat a boss, you have the opportunity to take their signature power and add it to your own deck.

| Boss Name | Circle | Primary Threat | Reward Ability |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Minos** | Limbo | Screen-wide lasers | Judgment Beam |
| **Cerberus** | Gluttony | Triple-lane charges | Triple-Strike Mod |
| **Elite Fiends** | Various | Rapid projectiles | Homing Spirit |

Effective combat requires mastering the dash. The most common cause of death is "dash exhaustion"—using the dash to move faster when no danger is present, only to have it on cooldown when a boss launches an unavoidable attack. Treat the dash as a defensive tool, not a mobility tool.
