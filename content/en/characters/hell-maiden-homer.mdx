<Section icon={Map} title="How to Rescue Homer in Limbo">

Unlocking **hell maiden homer** requires a successful excursion into the first circle of Hell. Unlike Virgil, who is met through early story progression in the Forum, Homer is trapped within the shifting sands of Limbo. Players must balance the need for experience collection with the exploration required to find her rescue trigger.

To ensure a successful rescue, you should focus on survivability in the first six minutes of the run. Once you have a stable four-weapon hand, you can begin searching for the Poet’s signature aura.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Enter Circle 1: Limbo">
    Start a standard run in Limbo. Ensure you are using a character you are comfortable with, such as Dante or Virgil, to maintain high mobility during the early waves.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Stabilize Your Spirit Cards">
    Collect experience orbs to fill all four weapon slots. Prioritize at least one area-of-effect weapon like the Wayfinder to keep the Brotchi and Skeleton hordes at bay while you explore.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Locate the Poet Trigger">
    Navigate the map to find the quest marker associated with the Poets of Limbo. You must interact with the environment and survive the intensified enemy waves that spawn during the rescue event.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Survive the 12-Minute Timer">
    The rescue is only finalized if you survive the full 12-minute wave timer in Limbo. If you fall before the timer ends, you will have to attempt the rescue in a subsequent run.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Visit the Poets' Forum">
    After a successful run, return to the hub area. Speak with Homer to exhaust her dialogue and permanently add her five unique abilities to your Spirit Card pool.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Rescue Priority">
Do not attempt the rescue during the final two minutes of the timer (10:00-12:00). The enemy density increases significantly, making it easy to get trapped while interacting with the Poet's soul.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Combat Tactics for Queen Minos">

Once you have secured **hell maiden homer** and her Winged Lyric, your next major hurdle is Queen Minos at the end of Limbo. This boss encounter tests your ability to maintain damage while navigating complex "bullet-hell" patterns. The Winged Lyric is particularly effective here because it allows you to focus 100% of your attention on Dante's movement.

Queen Minos cycles through telegraphed area attacks. With Homer’s weapon, you don't need to aim at the boss; you only need to survive the patterns.

| Attack Phase | Boss Action | Recommended Counter |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Phase 1** | Projectile Spirals | Circular movement around the arena edge |
| **Phase 2** | Telegraphed Slams | Save Dash for the final second of the telegraph |
| **Phase 3** | Summoned Minions | Use Poet Spell to clear space instantly |
| **Enraged** | Overlapping Patterns | Lateral movement and focused Winged Lyric fire |

<Callout type="info" title="Spell Usage">
Save Homer’s signature Spell for Phase 3. It provides a massive burst of single-target damage that can skip the most dangerous parts of the Queen's final enrage cycle.
</Callout>
