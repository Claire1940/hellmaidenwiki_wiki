The gothic horde-survival deckbuilder Hell Maiden reimagines Dante Alighieri's classic journey through the afterlife with a striking dark fantasy aesthetic. In the lore of the Divine Comedy, Lucan is one of the five great poets of antiquity residing in Limbo, alongside Virgil, Homer, Horace, and Ovid. Within the game, the figure of **hell maiden lucan** represents a highly anticipated companion character tied to the expanded Early Access roadmap.

Rescuing a Poet transitions them to the Poets' Forum, a hub area where players can engage in dialogue, advance character relationships, and receive unique Spirit Cards.

<Section icon={Map} title="How to Unlock Poets and Prepare for Lucan">

To prepare for the arrival of **hell maiden lucan** in a future content expansion, players must first master the rescue mechanics of the currently available Poets. Each Circle of Hell contains specific rescue objectives that must be completed during a standard 12-minute survival run. 

Failing to complete these rescue quests before the survival timer expires or before dying will force you to attempt the rescue again in a subsequent run. Use the following step-by-step progression route to unlock all active companions.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Interact with Virgil in the Forum">
    Begin your journey by speaking with Virgil in the Poets' Forum. Progressing through the early narrative chapters automatically unlocks Virgil's signature weapon, Wayfinder, and his screen-clearing Spell, Star Sign Pleiades.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Rescue Horace in Circle 1">
    Enter Circle 1 (Limbo) and locate Horace's rescue target. Complete the associated survival objective and defeat the surrounding horde to unlock Horace. This adds the Carpe Diem weapon and five new abilities to your permanent card pool.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Rescue Homer in Circle 1">
    Search for Homer within the Limbo Circle. Successfully completing her rescue objective unlocks the Winged Lyric weapon, a single-target offensive tool that never misses, making it highly effective against elite enemies.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Unlock Circle 2 and Save Ovid">
    Defeat Queen Minos to unlock Circle 2 (Lust). Navigate the faster enemy waves to locate and rescue Ovid. Saving Ovid unlocks the Codex Amoris card line, introducing powerful status-control abilities to your deck.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Survival Warning">
Poet rescue missions often spawn dense waves of elite enemies. Do not attempt a rescue until you have established a reliable area-of-effect weapon in your hand to prevent being overwhelmed.
</Callout>

Completing these rescues not only expands your gameplay options but also awards dedicated Steam achievements. Saving Horace and Homer unlocks the Poets of Limbo I achievement, while saving Ovid grants Poets of Limbo II.

</Section>

Deckbuilding in Hell Maiden requires balancing direct damage, area coverage, and passive modifiers. Your hand consists of up to four Weapon Cards, which can be enhanced by attaching and merging duplicate Mod Cards. 

To maximize your efficiency during high-difficulty runs in Limbo and Lust, consider utilizing one of the optimized deck archetypes outlined below.

Always prioritize merging duplicate Mod Cards to reach Level 3. A single Level 3 Mod is significantly more powerful than multiple low-level copies spread across different weapons.
</Callout>

Positioning your cards correctly within your hand is also crucial. Certain Mod Cards provide adjacent bonuses that only apply to weapons placed directly to their left or right. Always review your card layout after leveling up to ensure your primary damage weapons are receiving all available positional buffs.
