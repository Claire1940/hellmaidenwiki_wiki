<Section icon={Swords} title="Mastering the Card and Mod System">

The heart of **hell maiden ovid** lies in its sophisticated Mod card system. Every weapon you equip can be further enhanced by attaching Mod cards. These mods are color-coded, making it easier to identify their primary function during the chaotic heat of battle. Understanding how to layer these mods is the difference between a failed run and reaching Paradiso.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Boost Cards (Red)">
    - **Single Stat Buffs**
    - Increases base damage or speed
    - Essential for early-game scaling
  </Card>
  <Card title="Effect Cards (Green)">
    - **Status Ailments**
    - Applies Burn, Poison, or Slow
    - Best used with high-frequency weapons
  </Card>
  <Card title="Spread Cards (Blue)">
    - **Multi-Weapon Buffs**
    - Affects adjacent cards in your hand

  </Card>

Hand management is a critical skill. Because Spread cards (Blue) affect weapons to the left or right, the order in which you place your weapons in the four available slots matters. For instance, placing a weapon that benefits from high attack speed in the slot affected by a "Dextral Charge" card (which buffs the weapon to the right) can create devastating synergies.

| Mod Type | Background Color | Primary Function | Rarity Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Boost** | Red | Increases a single weapon stat | Common |
| **Effect** | Green | Adds extra effects like explosions | Common/Uncommon |
| **Spread** | Blue | Buffs multiple adjacent cards | Uncommon |
| **Miracle** | Yellow/Gold | Multiple powerful boosts | Rare |
| **Curse** | Purple | Massive power at a significant cost | Very Rare |

<Callout type="warning" title="The Risk of Curses">
Curse cards, like the **Curse of Dido**, can drastically increase your projectile count but may also randomly decrease it. Only take these if your current build has enough base damage to handle the occasional low-roll attack.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Card Merging and Evolution Strategy">

To survive the later stages of a run, you cannot rely on Level 1 cards. The merging system allows you to consolidate your power. When you have two identical cards of the same level, you can drag one over the other in your hand UI to evolve it.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Collect Identical Cards">
    Wait for the level-up screen to offer a card you already possess. Ensure it is the same level as the one in your hand.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Manual Merging">
    Open your player hand (Tab or E) and drag the new card onto the existing one. An orange glow indicates a valid merge.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Stat Eruption">
    Level 2 cards typically double the base stat, while Level 3 cards often add unique properties, such as a massive increase in projectile size or duration.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Hand Optimization">
    Once a card is Level 3, it can no longer be merged. Use the free slot to start building a new synergy or a second Level 3 mod.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Evolution Level | Visual Change | Typical Stat Bonus | Max Limit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1** | Standard Art | Base stats (e.g., +40% Damage) | N/A |
| **Level 2** | Silver Border | Enhanced stats (e.g., +60% Damage) | Requires 2x Lvl 1 |
| **Level 3** | Golden Glow | Elite stats (e.g., +100% Damage) | Requires 2x Lvl 2 |

<Callout type="success" title="The Power of Level 3 Mods">
A Level 3 **Proliferation** card adds three extra projectiles to a weapon. On a weapon like the Blazing Quill, this transforms a single shot into a wide-area volley that can clear entire waves of skeletons instantly.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Survival Tactics and Elite Boss Encounters">

Survival in **hell maiden ovid** requires more than just high damage; it requires spatial awareness. The environment in Limbo is cluttered with obstacles like stones and pillars that can snag your character. If you get stuck while a group of charging fire-worms or an Elite commander is nearby, your run will end abruptly.

**Key Survival Milestones:**
- **0-5 Minutes**: Focus on XP gain and movement speed.
- **5-10 Minutes**: Secure at least one Level 2 weapon and a defensive mod (like Slow or Knockback).
- **10+ Minutes**: You must have a Level 3 weapon or a full 4-weapon hand to handle the increased density of projectile-firing enemies.
