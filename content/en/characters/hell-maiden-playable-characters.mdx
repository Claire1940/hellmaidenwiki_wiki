Progression in the game is linear and story-driven. To expand your options and unlock the true potential of the **hell maiden playable characters** system, you must rescue the legendary Poets of Limbo. These poets include historical and mythological figures such as Virgil, Homer, Ovid, Horace, and Lucan.

Additionally, defeating the unique bosses at the end of each circle grants two new abilities. The narrative unfolds dynamically, with Poets offering new dialogues in the Forum hub area and bosses presenting fresh pre-fight interactions on repeat runs.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Deck-Building and Mod Card Mechanics">

The core of the game's customization lies in its deck-building mechanics. During a run, Dante can equip up to four weapons simultaneously. Leveling up offers two distinct pathways: Beatrice can descend to offer a direct stat charm, or you can draw cards from your pre-configured deck.

Unlike other survival games where passive items apply global modifiers to all weapons, this game utilizes localized Mod Cards. Mod Cards must be attached directly to specific Weapon Cards in your hand. This localized system allows for highly specialized weapon configurations, though some rare cards can apply adjacent bonuses to neighboring slots.

Mod Cards also introduce status effects and damage-over-time options, such as burning and poison, which can be stacked simultaneously on tough bosses. A highly praised feature is the visual weapon customization: applying elemental modifiers physically changes the appearance of Dante's attacks, such as turning Dragon Breath's flames purple when paired with poison.

| Status Effect | Visual Weapon Change | Combat Utility | Recommended Weapon Synergy |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Poison | Projectiles glow with a purple aura | Inflicts damage-over-time and weakens defense | Dragon Breath |
| Burn | Adds a fiery red trail to attacks | Deals high ticking damage that stacks | Selenic Bloom |
| Slow | Adds a frost-like trail to attacks | Reduces enemy movement speed significantly | Rapid-fire starting weapons |

<Callout type="warning" title="Mod Card Placement">
Because Mod Cards generally only affect the weapon they are physically attached to, you must plan your card layout carefully. Placing a speed modifier on a slow, high-damage weapon is often far more effective than spreading modifiers thin.
</Callout>
